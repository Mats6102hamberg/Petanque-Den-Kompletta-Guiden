Introduction

Building a boule court is more than a construction project ‚Äî it is creating a place for community, training, and competition. 1) Planning and preparation

Before the first excavation, work through this checklist carefully.

Placement checklist

  • Size: Is there enough space for the chosen court plus a safety zone around it?
  • Clear area: Allow at least 1 metre around the court for movement and spectators.
  • Slope: Preferably a gentle slope of 1‚Äì2% for drainage.
  • Sun and shade: A balance is ideal for comfort during long playing sessions.
  • Distance to buildings: At least 5 metres (noise and safety).
  • Drainage: Avoid low areas where water tends to collect.
  • Soil type: Firm ground is best; avoid clay and peat.

8) Tips from experienced court builders

  • Build one excellent court rather than two mediocre ones.
  • Invest time in the base layer ‚Äî it is the backbone of the court.
  • Always keep a rake next to the court.
  • Make the court slightly larger than you think you need.
  • Document the build ‚Äî it will help with future improvements.
