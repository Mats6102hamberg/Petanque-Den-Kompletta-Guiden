1) Manufacturing ‚Äî two main types of metal

Competition boules are almost always made of steel with a hollow center. Two main materials dominate:

Carbon Steel (acier carbon)

  • Has a slightly porous surface that provides excellent grip.
  • Very popular among experienced and professional players.
  • Often gives better "feel" both in the hand and on the ground.
  • Disadvantage: rusts more easily and requires regular maintenance.

Best for you if: you play often, want maximum feel, and are willing to care for your boules properly.

Stainless Steel (inox)

  • Smoother surface than carbon steel.
  • Almost maintenance-free ‚Äî rarely rusts.
  • Slightly less "bite" on the ground compared to carbon steel.
  • A very good choice for beginners and recreational players.

Best for you if: you want easy ownership, train irregularly, or play in humid conditions.

Semi-soft boules and Hardness+

Many modern boules are made with dual steel alloys, often called semi-soft or Hardness+.

These combine:

  • soft feel when pointing,
  • with sufficient durability for shooting.

They are very popular as all-round boules.

3) Hardness and surface texture

Boules are typically divided into three categories:

Type Feel Ground behavior Best for
Soft (tendre) Grip-friendly Stops quickly Pointers

Soft boules (tendre)

  • "Bite" better into the gravel.
  • Absorb impacts.
  • Roll less after landing.
  • Very good for pointing.

Hard boules (dure)

  • Roll further.
  • Bounce more on hard ground.
  • Transfer energy more clearly when shooting.
  • Often preferred by shooters.

Shooting boules (shooters)

Often slightly lighter, somewhat softer, and sometimes with a larger diameter to improve the hitting surface.

Common examples: MS Black Spin, Obut Match.

All-round boules

For players who both point and shoot. Often semi-soft ‚Äî a good compromise between feel and durability.

Common examples: Obut Zen, JB Pro Series.

6) Maintenance and care ‚Äî five simple steps

To keep your boules in good condition for many years:

  1. Clean after every game ‚Äî wash with warm water and a little dish soap.
  2. Dry thoroughly ‚Äî moisture causes rust.
  3. Oil lightly ‚Äî wipe with a lightly oiled cloth.
  4. Remove rust when needed ‚Äî use a fine steel brush or white wine vinegar, then rinse and oil.
  5. Store dry ‚Äî avoid damp basements and cold storage rooms.
