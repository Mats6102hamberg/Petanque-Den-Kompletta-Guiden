Part 4 • Training Cards

Mental Tools You Can Use Immediately

Perfect for mobile, sideline or pre-match routines

Each card has “When”, “Purpose” and concrete steps. Swipe-friendly on your phone, printable as PDF.

Quick Access

  • Bookmark this page on your phone.
  • Download the full PDF kit: Mental card kit (PDF).

🟥 Card 1 – Five Breaths Before the Deciding Throw

When: Before high-pressure throws.

Purpose: Lower pulse and let the body respond.

  1. Stand heavy in the circle.
  2. Inhale four seconds, exhale six seconds.
  3. Visualise the throw’s path during the breaths.
  4. Internal cue: “Steady hand – soft release”.

🟦 Card 2 – Reset After a Miss

When: Immediately after a miss or poor decision.

Purpose: Break negative spirals.

  1. Step out of the circle and shake the arm.
  2. Exhale silently, let the shoulders drop.
  3. Pick a new landing spot, see it clearly.
  4. Say: “New throw – new chance”.

🟩 Card 3 – My Three Values Under Pressure

When: Before practice or competition.

Purpose: Guide behaviour when it burns.

  1. Write three values (e.g., courage, presence, respect).
  2. Connect each value to one action.
  3. Let your signature become a promise to the team.
  4. Review the card between matches.

🟨 Card 4 – Focus at 12–12

When: Final end, deciding points.

Purpose: Keep process focus when everything is on the line.

  1. Repeat the routine slower than normal.
  2. Lock onto the landing point early.
  3. Team question: “What do we need right now?”
  4. Visualise the point after the throw.

🟪 Card 5 – Match Start Ritual

When: Before the opening throw of practice or competition.

Purpose: Enter with the same energy every time.

  1. Three paced breaths.
  2. Quick check: grip, posture, target.
  3. Team phrase: “We play our game”.
  4. First throw = process, not result.

⬛ Card 6 – Team Focus Under Pressure

When: The team becomes stressed, noisy or scattered.

Purpose: Maintain communication and energy.

  1. Quick round: each player states “what I take responsibility for next throw”.
  2. Agree on a signal that means “rewind” when someone rushes.
  3. Remind each other of the team values.
  4. End every end with a high five – regardless of result.
