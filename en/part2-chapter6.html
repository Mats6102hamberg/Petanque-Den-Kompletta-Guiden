Championship Shooting (au fer shots)

From basic shots to masterful techniques

A championship shooter masters several types of shots and knows when to use each technique to maximize the chance of winning.

Direct Hits vs. Rolling Shots

A championship shooter masters several types of shots. An "au fer shot" is a direct hit where your ball strikes the opponent's ball dead center; the goal is often to make a carreau, i.e., your ball takes the opponent's place and stays there.

Different Shot Types According to Obut:

  • Direct Shot: Knocks away the opponent's ball straight from the front
  • Rolling Shot: The ball is sent with force along the ground
  • Shot on the Jack: To kill the end
  • Shot in Front of Boule: The ball lands just in front of the opponent's and rolls in

When Should You Choose a Direct Shot?

Choose a direct shot when the terrain is uneven or when you need a reliable result ‚Äì a bouncing ball is less affected by the surface.

When Should You Choose a Rolling Shot?

Rolling shots work best on even courts or when you need to shoot several opponent balls in a row, but carry risk of bounces and deviations.

Training Tips to Become a Skilled Shooter

To shoot with power and precision requires technique and momentum. According to experts, the arm should function as a pendulum; think more about generating swing speed than tensing muscles.

The 6-Boule Challenge:

A popular exercise to improve your shooting technique:

  1. Place six balls as targets in specific positions
  2. Shoot one boule at a time focusing on replacing the target ball
  3. After each shot, step out of the circle
  4. Visualize the next throw
  5. Enter the circle again and shoot

Repeat the exercise at different distances to improve both power and precision.

Top Tips for Shooting Technique:

  • Keep fingers together ‚Äì gives better control of the ball
  • Stand firmly with leading foot pointing toward the target
  • Use the free arm for balance
  • Finish the throw with hand pointing toward the target

Additional Training Exercises

Shooting Gate with Bands:

Build a "shooting gate" with two bands at head height where you try to throw the ball between the bands. This technique helps you maintain a consistent throwing path and height.

Wiffle Ball Training:

Use a wiffle ball or plastic ball as a target to create a clear hitting surface and force yourself to throw with controlled height.

Analysis of Professional Shooting Styles

The best shooters have developed personal yet effective throws. Here are some of the most successful techniques:

Technique Description Result
Elbow Shot The ball is held near the face, the swing is brought back past the hip and then released at a steep angle The ball drops onto the target ball and replaces it ‚Äì perfect carreau
Lift and Drop The player lifts the ball to waist height, releases it along the throwing line with short backswing and early release Gives a soft but very precise shot
Wing Spread A high swinging throw with powerful release where the player uses a large arc Hits the target directly with great force
Crane School A grip variant where the ball is held further out in the fingers Generates much backspin ‚Äì suitable when you need more control
Fold and Roll A slower swing with moderate follow-through Used with heavier balls for controlled shots

The Importance of Consistent Height

Regardless of technique, professionals strive for consistent height. Analysis shows that world-class shooters almost always throw the ball so the top of the arc is around head height; at longer distances they throw slightly higher.

Training with Framework:

By practicing with a framework with two bands ‚Äì one at head height and one just above ‚Äì you can train yourself to throw between the bands and get an even trajectory.

Important: When shooters focus on the throw's height rather than hitting the target boule, accuracy often increases!

Summary

Becoming a championship shooter is about:

  • Mastering several different shot types
  • Choosing the right technique for each situation
  • Training systematically with focused exercises
  • Developing consistent throwing trajectory and height
  • Studying and learning from professional techniques
