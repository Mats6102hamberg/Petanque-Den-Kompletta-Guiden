Differences in Mindset and Strategy

In practice, the term "social" or "casual" p√©tanque is used for friendly games without referees, while "competition play" refers to sanctioned tournaments with referees.

Social Play:

  • Played without umpire (referee)
  • When something irregular happens, teams decide together
  • Atmosphere is relaxed and the goal is socializing
  • Players try new tactics and take turns throwing
  • Points are counted but aren't the most important

Competition Play:

  • There's a referee who is called for irregularities
  • Referee's decision cannot be appealed
  • Maintain tempo and follow one-minute rule for throws
  • Think strategically ‚Äì every point can decide the match
  • Prepare throw, discuss with teammates, read terrain

Informal Traditions and Etiquette

"Faire fanny"

Losing 0‚Äì13 is called "faire fanny". In some clubs it's tradition that the team making fanny buys opponents a drink or, according to old French custom, kisses a wooden statue depicting Fanny. It's of course a playful ritual showing how social the game is.

Good Etiquette Means:

  • Not walking in front of the player throwing
  • Standing still and quiet when someone is concentrating
  • Waiting to congratulate or comment until the ball has landed
  • Using approved instruments for measuring (not feet)
  • Showing respect to opponents, audience and referees

By combining official rule knowledge with good etiquette, you become an appreciated player wherever you throw your balls.
