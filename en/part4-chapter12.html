Mental Strength and Focus

Play your best game when everything is on the line

The difference between good and great players is usually mental. Mental strength can be trained exactly like technique.

Handling Pressure

Three techniques:

  1. Breathing and visualization: Deep breathing plus clear images calm the nervous system.
  2. Positive intent: Replace “don’t miss” with “follow the routine”.
  3. Rituals: The same preparation before every throw tells the body what will happen.

Two Choices Under Pressure

When the pulse rises, you either follow fear or you follow your values.

Avoidance play Value-driven play
Playing short, passive and waiting for opponent mistakes. Playing to win – trusting your plan even when it stings.
Focus on not doing wrong. Focus on doing the right thing right now.
Shallow breathing, no routine. Steady breathing, identical ritual every time.

Values that Guide Behaviour

  • Courage: I throw the difficult shot when it matters.
  • Presence: One throw at a time – land in the circle.
  • Stability: I keep my routine regardless of the score.
  • Respect: I lift my team, even after mistakes.

Training Tools for the Court

Tool 1 · “I Stand Here”

A quick grounding before important throws.

  • Let the feet sink into the circle.
  • Feel the weight of the boule; scan shoulders and hand.
  • Breathe in for four seconds, hold your gaze on the jack for two.
  • Internal cue: “Steady hand – one throw”.

Tool 2 · Reset After a Miss

Misses are the start of the next throw.

  1. Exhale slowly through your mouth.
  2. Shake the throwing arm, release tension.
  3. Select a new landing spot and picture it clearly.
  4. Say softly: “New throw – new chance”.

Tool 3 · Command Voice

The final thought before release controls the body.

Examples:

  • “Soft release.”
  • “Roll – stop.”
  • “Steady hand.”
  • “Just like training.”

Match Scenarios

When You Miss Several Throws

Impulse: throw harder. Solution: keep tempo, lock onto the landing spot, follow the routine.

When You Want to Impress

Narrow the world. You play for the circle, not the crowd. Eyes on the jack – not on the gallery.

When Victory Is Close

Switch “we’re about to win” to “one throw, then another”. Future thinking kills precision.
