Appendix B

Complete Rulebook

Official rules for pétanque

Introduction

Pétanque is part of the boule family – French games where you throw balls toward a small wooden ball. The game is popular, social and requires precision. Many learn the basics in minutes, but the rules have rich detail for competitions.

⚡ 10 Rules That Solve 90% of Situations

Learn these rules first – they cover the most common situations in the game:

# Rule What It Means
1 Both feet in circle You must stand with both feet completely within the circle until the ball has landed. Otherwise the throw is invalid.
2 Jack 6-10 meters Jack must be thrown at least 6m and max 10m from circle. At least 1m from obstacles/boundary.
3 Team without closest ball throws The team that does NOT have the ball closest to the jack continues throwing until they get the closest ball or run out of balls.
4 Points = number of balls closer After the end, count how many of your balls are closer to the jack than opponent's closest. That's your points.
5 First to 13 wins Match is played until one team reaches 13 points. No overtime – first to 13 wins directly.
6 Ball/jack over boundary = dead If a ball or jack completely crosses the boundary/goes outside playing area, it's dead and removed.
7 Dead jack = new end If jack becomes dead (over boundary/invisible) and no team has balls left, start new end from same circle. Same team throws jack.
8 Measure only when in doubt Measure only when impossible to see with eye which ball is closest. Use approved measuring instrument.
9 Don't touch balls before measuring Never move balls or jack before both teams agree on points. Otherwise you lose the end.
10 1 minute per throw In competition you have max 1 minute from when it's your turn until you throw. Otherwise warning/disqualification.

Basic Rules and Game Concept

Equipment

Boule:

  • Made of metal
  • Weighs 650–800 g
  • Diameter between 70.5 and 80 mm
  • Younger players may use smaller balls (minimum 600 g and 65 mm)
  • Must have engraved weight and manufacturer's mark

Cochonnet/Jack:

  • Small ball that is the target
  • Made of wood or approved synthetic material
  • Diameter around 30 mm
  • Weighs 10–18 g
  • Must not be magnetic

Game Formats and Team Setup

Game Format Players per Team Boules per Player
Singles 1 3
Doubles 2 3
Triples 3 2

Court and Playing Area

  • International courts: Minimum 15 × 4 meters
  • National/regional courts: 12 × 3 meters
  • Boundaries (lines or strings) mark the court's length
  • If a ball or jack completely crosses the boundary it becomes dead

Starting the Game

  1. Teams flip a coin to choose court or starting throw
  2. The winning team draws a circle on the ground (diameter 35–50 cm)
  3. A player from that team throws the jack 6–10 meters
  4. The jack must be at least 1 meter from obstacles and boundaries
  5. The same team throws the first boule

Playing an End (Mène)

After the first boule is thrown, the opposing team throws until they get a boule closer to the jack or run out of boules. Then it's the first team's turn again. This continues until all boules are played.

Important:

  • The team without the closest boule always throws
  • If teams are tied (equal distance), the team that threw last continues
  • Players must keep both feet in the circle until the boule lands
  • Boules are thrown palm down to give backspin

Scoring

When all boules are played, measure which team has boules closest to the jack. That team scores one point for each of their boules that is closer than the opponent's closest boule.

Example: If Team A has three boules within 20 cm of the jack and Team B's closest is 25 cm away, Team A scores 3 points.

Starting a New End

The team that scored draws a new circle where the jack landed and throws the jack for the next end. The game continues until one team reaches 13 points.

Special Situations

Dead Jack

If the jack goes out of bounds or becomes invisible:

  • If both teams still have boules, the end is void and replayed from the same circle
  • If only one team has boules left, they score one point per remaining boule
  • The team that threw the jack in the void end throws it again

Dead Boule

A boule that goes completely out of bounds is dead and removed from play. It cannot score points.

Measuring

  • Measure only when you cannot see with the eye which boule is closest
  • Use an approved measuring tool (string, measuring tape, caliper)
  • Never use feet or body parts to measure
  • If boules are exactly equal distance, no points are scored for those boules

Competition Rules

Time Limits

In official competitions, players have maximum 1 minute from when it's their turn until they throw. Exceeding this results in a warning, and repeated violations can lead to disqualification.

Etiquette and Conduct

  • Stand still and quiet when opponent is throwing
  • Don't walk between player and jack
  • Wait to remove boules until both teams agree on points
  • Show respect to opponents, audience and referees
  • Don't touch or move boules before measuring

Referee Decisions

In competitions, a referee is present to resolve disputes. The referee's decision is final and cannot be appealed.

Summary

Pétanque is a game that combines precision, strategy and social interaction. With these basic rules you can start playing immediately. As you gain experience, you'll learn the finer points and special situations that make the game even more interesting.

Remember: The most important rule is to have fun and show good sportsmanship. Pétanque is as much about the social experience as it is about winning!
